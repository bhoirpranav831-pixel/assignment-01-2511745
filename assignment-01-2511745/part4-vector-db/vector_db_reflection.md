## Vector DB Use Case

Keyword search is not an effective way to search legal document especially since the search is performed on a word by word basis. In legal documents different terms are used to refer to the same clauses or sections such as termination clauses, terms, or conditions or liability clauses, terms, or conditions. Therefore a keyword search might not return relevant results especially if the keywords are not used in the document being searched.

A vector database is an effective way to search legal documents especially since the search is performed on a sentence or phrase basis which is more meaningful in terms of the context in which the terms are used. In a vector database documents are stored in a high-dimensional space which is a numerical representation of the document. Therefore  query entered in natural language will also be represented in a numerical format which can be compared to the document vectors to determine their similarity. Therefore a query such as "termination clauses" can return relevant results such as clauses related to the ending conditions of a contract even if they are not referred to in the query or in the document being searched.
